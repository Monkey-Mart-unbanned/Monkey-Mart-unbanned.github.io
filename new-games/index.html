Latest games on Monkey Mart

Welcome to the new games page. Every game listed here is a recent addition to Monkey Mart, picked because it runs in the browser, plays smoothly on a school laptop, and does not need a download. Click any card above to jump straight into the game. There is nothing to install, no sign-up wall, and no pop-ups blocking the first minute of play.

What kind of games live here?

The catalog leans toward casual and management titles. Think clickers where you tap to harvest crops, idle games where helpers do the work while you plan the next upgrade, and small simulation games that fit in a short break. Most of them are designed to be picked up in seconds but kept open in a second tab while you do other things. That is the point of this page: a place where you always have a fresh option to try.

Monkey Mart

Monkey Mart is the flagship title. You run a small supermarket run entirely by monkeys. Plant seeds, harvest them, drop the goods on shelves, and ring up customers. As money rolls in, you unlock new products, hire helpers, and expand the floor plan. The original game page has the full FAQ, a strategy breakdown, and the embed. The Monkey Mart game lives at the root of the site.

Bunny Market

Bunny Market is the second title, set on a sunny island where a small bunny builds a trading post. Walk around to gather wood and stone, build stalls, and watch customers line up. It is more relaxed than Monkey Mart and leans into the idle side of management. Open the Bunny Market page for the controls and full walkthrough.

How to pick a new game

If you want to stay busy with your hands, start with Monkey Mart. The clicking loop is fast and the upgrades are constant. If you want something that runs in the background while you read, Bunny Market is the better fit. Both work on a phone, a Chromebook, or an old desktop. The cards above are ordered by recency, so whatever is newest sits at the front of the row.

Browser and device support

All games on this page run in modern browsers. Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari on desktop, plus the built-in browsers on iOS and Android, all work without plugins. There is no Flash, no Java, and no extra launcher. If a game is stuttering, the most common fix is closing other tabs so the browser can free up memory. Most of the games also have a fullscreen button inside the play area.
